The **OpenSquawk** name, logo and branding are trademarks of the OpenSquawk project. The code is open
source; the brand is not.

## What you may do

- **You may fork the code** and self-host it under the terms of the [AGPLv3](LICENSE).
- You may modify it, run it privately, and build on top of it.
- You may state, factually and without implying endorsement, that your work is
  "based on OpenSquawk" or "powered by OpenSquawk Core".

## What you may not do without permission

- **You may not use the OpenSquawk name, logo or branding for a competing hosted service.**
  Running an "OpenSquawk Cloud", an "OpenSquawk Cloud alternative", or any hosted product that presents
  itself as OpenSquawk, requires written permission.
- You may not use the marks in a way that suggests official status, endorsement or affiliation.
- You may not use the marks in your product name, domain, company name or app store listing.

The goal is simple: forks and self-hosting are welcome, but nobody should be able to spin up a look-alike
"OpenSquawk" hosted service and dilute the project or confuse users.
