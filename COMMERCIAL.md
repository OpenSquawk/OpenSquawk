# Commercial Use

OpenSquawk Core is open-source software, but OpenSquawk is also a commercial product.
This document explains where the line is.

## What the open-source license covers

You may **use and modify OpenSquawk Core under the [AGPLv3](LICENSE)**. That includes:

- Self-hosting the core training engine for basic radio training
- Modifying the code, prompts and controller logic for your own use
- Building plugins and scenarios on top of the SDK and connectors

If you run a modified version as a network service, the AGPLv3 requires you to make your modified
source available to its users. See the [LICENSE](LICENSE) for the exact terms.

## What requires a paid plan or separate agreement

Commercial use of the **official OpenSquawk hosted platform, premium scenarios, brand assets, cloud
services and instructor features requires a paid plan**. Specifically:

- **Hosted OpenSquawk Cloud** — managed hosting, accounts, billing, teams and usage limits
- **AI feedback / debriefing** and the VATSIM-ready score service
- **Premium voice models** and **premium scenario packs**
- **Instructor / commercial** usage (teaching with OpenSquawk, using it inside a paid offering)

These live in separate, private repositories and are **not** licensed under the AGPLv3.
